Pharmacy Specialities Recognized by the Pharmacy and Poisons Board (PPB) of Kenya

The Pharmacy and Poisons Board (PPB) of Kenya has outlined various specialities in pharmacy to address the growing complexities in the pharmaceutical sector. These specialities ensure the availability of highly skilled pharmacists in specialized areas of practice. Below are the recognized specialities and their requirements:

1. Pharmacotherapy

  • Focus: Safe, appropriate, and economical use of medicines.
  • Requirements:
    • Bachelor of Pharmacy (BPharm) degree.
    • Master of Pharmacy (MPharm) in Clinical Pharmacy (2 years minimum).
    • 1-year post-master residency.
    • Pass Board Certified Qualifying Examination.

2. Clinical Pharmacy

  • Focus: Patient-centered care, enhancing patient outcomes through rational drug therapy.
  • Requirements:
    • BPharm degree.
    • Master of Pharmacy in Clinical Pharmacy (2 years minimum).
    • 1-year post-master residency.
    • Pass Board Certified Qualifying Examination.

3. Public Health Pharmacy

  • Focus: Disease prevention, health promotion, and policy formulation.
  • Requirements:
    • BPharm degree.
    • Master’s in Public Health or related fields.
    • 1-year residency or equivalent work experience.
    • Pass Board Certified Qualifying Examination.

4. Community Pharmacy

  • Focus: Advanced pharmaceutical services and health promotion in community settings.
  • Requirements:
    • BPharm degree.
    • Master of Pharmacy in Community Pharmacy or related field (2 years minimum).
    • 2 years post-master experience.
    • Pass Board Certified Qualifying Examination.

5. Regulatory Affairs

  • Focus: Ensuring compliance with pharmaceutical regulations and laws.
  • Requirements:
    • BPharm degree.
    • Master’s in Regulatory Affairs or related fields.
    • 2 years post-master experience.
    • Pass Board Certified Qualifying Examination.

6. Natural Therapy

  • Focus: Use of natural remedies and complementary medicine.
  • Requirements:
    • BPharm degree.
    • Master of Pharmacy in Pharmacognosy or related fields (2 years minimum).
    • 2 years of relevant experience or completion of a fellowship.
    • Pass Board Certified Qualifying Examination.
